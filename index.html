Welcome to Hítará

Hítará

Hítará river is located in Hítardalur valley about 100km from Reykjavik city. The river has 4 tributaries, Stekká, Grjótá, Kverná and Melsá running in the main river throughout the valley.

Hítardalur is quite spectacular with lava, birch forests and canyons with beautiful waterfalls.

the river

Hítará river originates from the lake Hítarvatn in Hítardalur valley. The river runs through old lava down the valley until it enteres the waterfall in the middle of the valley. Below the valley we have the tributaries Stekká and Grjótá joining the Hítará river. This part of the river is called Hítára 2/Grjótá. Grjótá is sold as a special beat with a self-catering lodge with 2 rods.

After the junction the river is called "Tálmi" and is part of the main beat. Tálmi enters the main beat just above the main road to Snæfellsnes peninsula. Hítará river runs after that about 10 km to the ocean.

The Hítará beat is fished with 6 rods from middle of June to middle of September. Our short season and limited number of guests creates high demand, so we encourage you to contact us in good time if you wish to come and experience Hítará river.

the lodge

The lodge "Lundur" overlooking the scenic "Breiðin" pool is one of the oldest lodges in Iceland with great history. The lodge was built by the famous wrestler Jóhannes Jósepsson or "Jóhannes á Borg" around 1950.

Jóhannes was the owner of Hotel Borg located in Reykjavik Parliament square. Jóhannes was fishing in Hítárá from 1940 to 1964 with friends. The lodge was renovated in 1996 and again in 2018 when guest rooms were built besides the lodge. In the lodge are large flocks of stuffed birds.

Not your average fishing logde.

1.

Hítará is a beautiful salmon river with good runs of grilse and good numbers of multi sea winter salmon in the 10 - 18lb class. Hítará has a good number of sea trout and arctic char, some sea trout up to 85cm.

2.

Hítará is the main river, originating from lake Hítarvatn. In the middle of the Hítardalur valley Stekká, Grjótá, Kverná and Melsá enter Hítará. The main river is fished with 6 rods and the upper beat is fished with 2 rods.

3.

There is an upper beat Hítará 2/Grjótá with 2 rods and has a self catering lodge with four rooms. The upper beat is versatile with slower current in the Tálmi area and small waterfalls and deeper pools in Grjótá. Good numbers of arctic char are caught in this area.

4.

The rivers are ideal for light 6-8 weight rods, small flies and tubes and hitched flies on floating lines. Sometimes a switch rod is needed in high water conditions and in the lower part of the river.
